# Budget Buddy — Intro for Fractional CMO

*This is a working doc, not polished copy. We're handing this to you at the "app is almost done, now what" stage. We haven't figured out positioning, pricing, or go-to-market yet. That's partly why you're here.*

---

## What is this?

Budget Buddy is a personal finance app for people who are serious about their money. Not casual budgeters. Not people who want gamification and confetti. People who have read the books, are doing the work, and want a tool that treats them like adults.

The app does five things:

1. **Transaction tracking** — Connect bank accounts via Plaid, or import CSV files. Transactions get auto-classified using a trained ML model. You can correct classifications and the model learns from that.

2. **Budget management** — Set monthly budgets by category. The app shows you where you stand in real time, week by week, with a calculated "adjusted weekly target" that accounts for known upcoming expenses.

3. **Month-end review** — A structured three-act flow: (1) face your numbers honestly, (2) reflect on why you spent what you spent, (3) make a concrete plan for next month. This is the core experience — we think most finance apps skip this entirely.

4. **Sinking funds** — Dedicated savings buckets for irregular expenses: car repairs, travel, annual subscriptions, taxes. The app tracks contributions and shows you whether you're on track.

5. **Buddy AI** — An AI coach (powered by Claude) that knows your numbers. It generates weekly insights, answers questions in a chat interface, and guides the month-end review. The voice is direct, slightly jaded, and non-performative. It does not cheer you on. It tells you the deal.

---

## Who is it for?

People who are intentional about their finances. They're typically:

- 25–45
- Above-median income, often not spending like it (intentionally)
- Already using some tool — YNAB, Copilot, spreadsheets — but feel like they're not getting enough signal from it
- Data-driven but not nerds about it. They want insights surfaced, not raw exports.
- Frustrated by apps that feel like they're designed for people who just discovered credit cards

We haven't validated this ICP rigorously yet. That's honest.

---

## What makes it different?

A few things we believe but haven't pressure-tested in the market:

**The month-end review is the product.** Every other finance app is primarily a dashboard. We think the real value is the structured reflection and planning loop — doing it every month, consistently, guided. The dashboard exists to support the review, not the other way around.

**No anxiety design.** Most finance apps use red for spending, which creates low-grade panic every time you open the app. We use muted purple for expenses. Red is only for "you've exceeded your budget." This is intentional and we think it matters for retention — people don't avoid the app because it makes them feel bad.

**Buddy is a character, not a chatbot.** The AI has a specific voice: smart, slightly jaded, deeply caring. It says "you spent $400 on dining this month, which is $120 over. here's the pattern i'm seeing." Not "Great job on your savings goal! 🎉". We think this registers differently — more like a trusted advisor than a product trying to keep you engaged.

---

## What it is NOT

- Not a wealth management or investment platform. No brokerage integrations, no portfolio tracking yet.
- Not an enterprise or team product. Single-user, personal finance.
- Not a mobile app yet. Web only. Mobile is on the roadmap but not V1.
- Not built for people who want to be told everything is fine.

---

## Where we are

V1 is nearly done. The core flows work — transaction import, classification, budgeting, sinking funds, the three-act review, and Buddy chat are all built and functional. We're in a frontend design pass right now, getting the visual system right before we show it to real users.

We have not done:
- Any paid marketing
- Formal user research (some informal feedback from friends/family)
- Pricing decisions
- Positioning or messaging work
- A clear distribution strategy

We're open to the app being free with a paid tier, flat subscription, or freemium. We haven't decided. We're small enough that the right early strategy might be completely manual — direct outreach to FIRE communities (r/financialindependence, ChooseFI community, etc.) rather than paid acquisition.

---

## Questions we'd want a CMO's take on

1. Who's the sharpest ICP? We have a gut sense of "intentional, serious budgeters" but haven't stress-tested whether that's a real addressable segment or just a vibe.
2. The month-end review is our core differentiator — is that something people will understand as a selling point before they've experienced it?
3. What's the right early channel? Personal finance communities feel like the obvious answer but we'd want to stress-test that.
4. The AI angle (Buddy) — is that a lead feature in positioning right now, or does it create noise given how saturated "AI + everything" is?
5. Should we be building in public? The product has a strong point of view. That might lend itself to founder-led content.
